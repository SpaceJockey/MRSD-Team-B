--- title: Software Architecture Overvew date: Nov 20, 2013, 8:47 AM author: Brian Boyle --- Pictured below is a high-level diagram of our software architecture for the Space Jockey Robot, depicting the movement of information through ROS nodes and topics in the form of messages. A more detailed description follows below.

Information Flow Overview

  1. The user issues commands through the G.U.I which are published to the "sched_command" node
  2. Two nodes subscribe to "sched_command", the Scheduler and the Gait Generator
    1. Urgent commands that directly manage the scheduler, such as Halt, Resume, or Clear, are picked up by the Scheduler node
    2. Commands that command the robot to move to user-defined positions, or perform part of a gait sequence, are picked up by the Gait Generator
    3. The Gait Generator creates joint trajectories, which are sent to "sched_joints", which is subscribed to by the Scheduler
  3. The Scheduler queues commands from the Gait Generator, which it issues to "serial_link"
  4. An Arduino sketch within the Robot acts as a node which subscribes to "serial_link" and receives joint commands from the scheduler
  5. The Robot publishes its latest pose to the "joint_states" topic
  6. Both the G.U.I. and Rviz update their respective representations of the robot using information from "joint_states"
